Artemis Mission Highlights

Explore five key visuals from the Artemis missions, each representing a milestone or technology.

Space Launch System Rocket

This massive spacecraft is capable of taking the Orion astronauts and cargo to moon in the same mission. It is still under development and will be the most powerful rocket system once launched.

Orion Spacecraft

This is the NASA spacecraft that will carry the crew from our planet to the Moon.

The Human Landing System

Built with the help of Blue Origins and SpaceX, this landing system will be the final mode of transportation to the lunar surface. It is capable of carrying upto 30,000 pounds of cargo. Isn't that alot!

Gateway

This spaceship will be the one waiting in lunar orbit for the first crew to arrive for the lunar mission. It will remain in the orbit for than a decade , acting as space for humans to live and work, supporting long-term science and exploration on Moon.

Surface Mobility

NASA is in the process of developing new-generation spacesuits. These will help the astronauts move on the lunar surface during EVAs.Explore more about them in the game!

Artemis Mission Projects

These are some of the teams and projects are responsible for the core research going on at NASA about space habitats:

  • NextSTEP: The NASA Next Space Technologies for Exploration Partnerships (NextSTEP) was released in April 2016, with a focus on developing a habitation capability in cis-lunar space through multiple commercial partnerships
  • NASA Human Research Program (HRP): NASA’s Human Research Program (HRP) conducts research to understand and reduce the risks to astronaut health and performance during long-duration space missions.
  • Constellation Architecture Team-Lunar Habitat : The Constellation Architecture Team was a NASA group formed in the mid-2000s to define and integrate the mission architecture, vehicles, and technologies for the Constellation Program (the precursor to Artemis)
